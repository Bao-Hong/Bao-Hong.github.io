1. Ph.D. Research Projects

Smooth pursuit task: Participants were instructed to track the step–ramp motion of a red dot.

Serial Dependence in Ocular Tracking

My doctoral research investigates how recent sensory history shapes current perception, combining psychophysics, eye-tracking, EEG, and computational modeling.

  • 1.1 Serial Dependence in Smooth Pursuit of Preadolescent Children and Adults (2021–2024)
    Used eye-tracking to investigate whether ocular tracking exhibits serial dependence and its developmental aspects.
    Presented at the 2023 China Vision Science Conference (Talk, Zhejiang, China, Slides); Published in Investigative Ophthalmology & Visual Science (2024).[Link]


    Behavioral evidence of serial dependence in ocular tracking.

  • 1.2 Temporal Dynamics of Serial Effects in Ocular Tracking (2023.10–present)
    Used eye-tracking and computational modeling to investigate attraction–repulsion dynamics of previous information in ocular tracking.
    Presented at the Vision Sciences Society Annual Meeting (VSS 2024) (Talk, Florida, USA, Slides); Manuscript to be submitted.


    Attraction–repulsion dynamics revealed by Temporal observer model.

  • 1.3 Neural Evidence of Serial Dependence in Ocular Tracking (2024.10–present)
    Used EEG and eye-tracking to investigate how trial-history information is represented in visual cortical activity and modulated by alpha-band oscillations.
    Abstract to be presented at the Society for Neuroscience Annual Meeting (SfN 2025) (Poster, San Diego, USA); Manuscript to be submitted.


    Neural decoding reveals rhythmic representation of previous motion direction.

2. Supervised Graduate Research Projects

optic flow stimuli: A dot cloud that simulates forward self-movement.

Heading Perception

My supervised graduate research focuses on heading perception — how the visual system processes self-motion direction (heading) and how past experience shapes this perception.

  • 2.1 Center Bias in Forward and Backward Heading Perception (2024.9–present )
    Used psychophysics and Bayesian modeling to investigate how locomotion experience shapes asymmetric priors in heading perception.
    Presented at the 2025 China Experimental Psychology Conference (Talk, Chengdu, China, Slides) and the 2024 China Vision Science Conference (Poster, Guangzhou, China).
    Role: Led the project from inception, including experimental design, supervision of data collection, data analysis (psychometric fitting, modeling), and manuscript drafting.


    Ecological experience shapes heading perception.

  • 2.2 Temporal Dynamics of Heading and Scene-Relative Object Motion Judgments (2024.2–present)
    Used psychophysics to investigate how the precision of heading and scene-relative object motion judgments changes with optic flow duration.
    Presented at the 2024 China Vision Science Conference (Poster, Guangzhou, China).
    Role: Led the project from conception through execution: designed the experiment, supervised data collection and analysis, drafted the manuscript, and facilitated discussions on subsequent research directions.


    Temporal dynamics of heading and flow parsing precision with increasing optic flow duration.

3. Collaborative Projects

  • 3.1 Visual Discomfort in Head-Mounted Displays (2021, Industry Collaboration)
    This project examined and quantified how prolonged viewing through a head-mounted display (HMD) leads to visual discomfort.
    Conducted in collaboration with an industry partner to improve VR ergonomics and user experience.
    Role: Contributed to programming (VR eye-tracking using Vizard), data collection, and data analysis.
  • 3.2 Brain Maturation and Development of Ocular Tracking in Children (2020–2024)
    This research aims to examine the development of ocular tracking ability (eye-tracking) and the brain structural (structural MRI) changes with age.
    Presented at the Society for Neuroscience Annual Meeting (SfN 2025) (Poster, Chicago, USA), the 2023 China Experimental Psychology Conference (Poster, Zhejiang, China) and the 2023 China Vision Science Conference (Poster, Zhejiang, China).
    Role: Contributed to stimulus design and programming, data collection, and data analysis.

4. Undergraduate research (Early Work)

  • 4.1 Parallel Processing of Optic Flow and Object Motion (2020, Undergraduate thesis)
    Used a dual-task psychophysical paradigm to examine whether heading and object motion are processed independently during self-movement.
    Results found that increasing the difficulty of one task did not affect performance in the other, revealing parallel processing of heading and object motion signals.


    Parallel Processing of Optic Flow and Object Motion.

  • 4.2 Implicit Associations Between Power Concepts and Vertical Space (2019)
    Used the Implicit Relational Assessment Procedure (IRAP) to examine implicit associations between power concepts and vertical spatial metaphors.
    Findings demonstrated faster congruent responses for high–up and low–down pairings, supporting the embodied mapping of abstract concepts.
    Published in Frontiers in Psychology (2019). [Link]
